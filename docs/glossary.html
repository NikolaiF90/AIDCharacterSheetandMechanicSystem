Reference

Glossary

Terms used throughout CSMS documentation and in-game notifications.

A

act_ prefix

A keyword prefix placed before an action word in player input to trigger a mechanic. The prefix and keyword are stripped before the AI sees the input — the AI narrates naturally while the script tracks the mechanical consequence. Examples: act_rest, act_eat, act_read.

Adventure

An AI Dungeon story session. CSMS state persists across all actions within an adventure via AID's state object.

---

C

Caller

The character executing an action or Ordinance. In Ordinance commands, the caller is the first argument: /csms ordinance/Caller/OrdinanceName/Target. CSMS detects the caller automatically from player input in multiplayer.

Character Sheet

A 📋 story card created and managed by CSMS for a character. Contains stats, HP, level, XP, proficiency, and links to inventory and ordinances in the Notes field.

Config Card

The ⚙️ CSMS CFG story card. Auto-created on first action. World makers edit its Notes field to override config values live without touching the script.

CSMS

Character Sheet and Mechanics System. The script this documentation covers.

---

D

DAMAGING

The Ordinance classification for moves that deal HP damage. The AI determines whether an Ordinance is DAMAGING or NONDAMAGING based on the card entry. DAMAGING Ordinances trigger a dice roll before narration, and the script scans the AI's output for damage words to apply HP.

Development Points (DP)

Points awarded to characters on level up. Players spend DP to increase stats via /csms dp/STR:2,INT:1. Unspent DP carries over. NPCs have DP auto-allocated by the script.

---

H

Hook

One of three execution points in an AID script: Input (before the AI sees player text), Context (before the AI generates), Output (after the AI generates). CSMS runs logic in all three hooks depending on the operation.

---

I

iv_ prefix

The legacy inventory prefix from v1.9.0. Replaced by act_ in v2.0.0 as the universal action prefix. Both still work in v2.0.0.

---

M

Module

A self-contained feature of CSMS that can be toggled on or off independently via the config. Current modules: CHARACTER_SHEETS, COMBAT, ORDINANCE, INVENTORY, LEVELING.

---

N

NONDAMAGING

The Ordinance classification for moves that don't deal direct HP damage — buffs, utility, focus techniques, social maneuvers. A roll may still be involved depending on the Ordinance entry, but no damage scan is performed.

NotifyThem

The notification system bundled with CSMS. Outputs bracketed messages to the player after each action. The AI ignores brackets — notifications are purely player-facing. Built as a standalone system, usable independently of CSMS.

---

O

Ordinance

A named special ability or technique defined as a story card by the world maker. Executed via /csms ordinance/ command. The script feeds the card entry to the AI, rolls dice, reads the narrative output, and applies mechanical consequences. See the Ordinance guide.

---

P

Pending State

A tracked multi-step operation in progress. Ordinance execution and roll resolution both use pending states to track which step they're on across multiple AI responses. Pending states auto-expire if a step doesn't advance after a configured number of actions.

Proficiency Bonus

A bonus applied to certain rolls and checks, increasing as the character levels up. Thresholds are configurable via PROFICIENCY_THRESHOLDS in config.

---

S

Seat

An index position in the state.csmsPlayers array. Each multiplayer player occupies a seat. Used to track identity across name changes.

Story Card

A persistent card in AI Dungeon that injects content into the AI's context when triggered by keywords. CSMS uses story cards for character sheets, config, debug log, and Ordinances.

---

T

Tag System

Adding [CSMS] to a story card title to mark it for automatic sheet generation. When the character appears in story history, CSMS generates a sheet from the card's description. See the Modules page.

Target

The character on the receiving end of an Ordinance. Optional for NONDAMAGING Ordinances. Must have a 📋 character sheet to receive damage.

---

W

World Maker

The person who sets up and configures a CSMS-powered AI Dungeon scenario. Responsible for installing the script, writing Ordinance cards, configuring modules, and designing the adventure. Distinct from the player who plays in it.

---

X

XP (Experience Points)

Points awarded automatically through play. When XP reaches the XP_THRESHOLD, the character levels up. Sources and amounts are individually configurable. See the Leveling module.
