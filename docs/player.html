Player Guide

Everything you need to know as a player in a CSMS-powered adventure.

What is CSMS?

CSMS is a script running behind the scenes in your AI Dungeon adventure. It gives your character a proper stat sheet, tracks your inventory, handles dice rolls, and lets you trigger Ordinances — all without interrupting the story.

You don't need to understand how it works. You just need to know how to use it.

Your Character Sheet

Your character sheet lives in the Story Cards panel. Look for a card with a 📋 emoji at the start of the title — that's yours.

It looks like this:

Name: Kira | Level: 1 | XP: 0
HP: 10/10 | AC: 10 | Speed: 30ft
Proficiency: +2

STR: 10 (+0)  DEX: 10 (+0)  CON: 10 (+0)
INT: 10 (+0)  WIS: 10 (+0)  CHA: 10 (+0)

The AI sees this card when writing your story. If your STR is 18, the AI knows you're incredibly strong. If your HP is low, the AI knows you're in danger.

Creating Your Character

If the world maker hasn't set up automatic character creation, create your sheet manually:

/csms create/YourName

Your sheet will appear immediately.

Multiplayer: In adventures with Third Person mode enabled, your sheet is created automatically when you join. No command needed.

Editing Your Stats

Open the Story Cards panel, find your 📋 card, and edit it directly. Change any value you want — the script reads your changes on the next action automatically.

To force an immediate update after editing:

/csms update/stats/YourName
Warning: If you type something that isn't a valid number, the script rejects that value and keeps the old one. You'll see a notification explaining what was rejected.

Inventory

Your inventory lives in the Notes field of your character sheet card — the description area below the stats. It looks like this:

[Inventory]
- leather jacket
- burner phone
- $200 cash
- pocket knife
- notes "found near the docks"

Edit it freely — add items, remove them, annotate with notes "...". When you're done, sync it:

/csms update/inventory/YourName
Format rules: Each item gets its own - item line. Duplicate items are separate lines — no stacking. The notes "..." annotation is optional and preserved as-is.

Rolling Dice

When you want a mechanical outcome for an action, use the roll command:

/csms roll/pick the lock
/csms roll/convince the guard to let you through

CSMS asks the AI which stat fits best, rolls 1d20 + modifier, and shows you the breakdown:

[You initiated a roll: pick the lock
1d20+DEX = [14] + DEX(+2) = 16]

The result feeds into the AI's next response — so the story reflects what actually happened, not what the AI guessed.

Tip: You can combine a roll with normal text. You work carefully at the lock. /csms roll/pick the lock — the AI sees your action, CSMS handles the roll.

Ordinances

Ordinances are special behaviors written by the world maker that change how the world responds. Trigger one with:

/csms ordinance/OrdinanceName/TargetName

For example — an Ordinance called Quick Strike against a Guard:

/csms ordinance/Quick Strike/Guard

CSMS handles the rest — determining if a roll is needed, rolling it, applying damage if the move lands, and letting the AI narrate based on what actually happened.

What counts as an Ordinance? Ask your world maker. Ordinances can be combat moves, faction abilities, world laws, or anything else they've defined. Each one has a card in the Story Cards panel.

What the Stats Mean

StatWhat it affects
STRPhysical power, melee attacks, carrying capacity
DEXAgility, ranged attacks, stealth, reflexes
CONEndurance, health, resistance to pain
INTKnowledge, memory, reasoning
WISPerception, intuition, willpower
CHAPersuasion, deception, social influence
HPHow much damage you can take before going down
ACHow hard you are to hit

The number in brackets after each stat (like +2 or -1) is your modifier — this is what gets added to rolls.

Notifications

Sometimes you'll see a message in brackets at the top of an AI response:

[!NOTIFICATION!
You initiated a roll: pick the lock
1d20+DEX = [14] + DEX(+2) = 16]

This is CSMS talking to you. The AI ignores these brackets entirely — they're purely for you.

Commands Cheat Sheet

CommandWhat it does
/csms create/[name]Create a new character sheet

/csms stats/[name]Refresh a specific character's sheet
/csms update/stats/[name]Sync card stat edits to state
/csms update/inventory/[name]Sync inventory edits to state
/csms roll/[action]Roll dice for an action
/csms ordinance/[name]/[target]Trigger an Ordinance against a target
/csms reset/[name]Remove a specific character sheet
/csms resetRemove all character sheets
/csms cleanupRemove orphaned story cards

Frequently Asked Questions

My sheet disappeared. What do I do?

Type /csms stats to refresh it. If it's truly gone, type /csms create/YourName to recreate it. Your stats will reset to defaults — edit them back manually.

Can I set my stats to whatever I want?

Yes — CSMS doesn't judge. The world maker may have set a maximum value (default is 50), but within that range you're free to set anything.

My stat edit isn't sticking. Why?

Check that you entered a plain number — no letters or symbols. Also make sure the card structure is intact — the script reads values by position, so the format needs to stay consistent.

I see raw stat text appear in the story. Is that a bug?

This can happen if the tag system is generating a character sheet and something interrupted the process. It should only happen once and won't affect your story. Just keep playing.

The AI isn't respecting my stats. What's wrong?

Make sure you've run /csms update/stats/YourName after any edits. If the sheet looks correct but the AI still ignores it, the world maker may need to check their context injection settings.
