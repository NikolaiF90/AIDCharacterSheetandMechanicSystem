Ordinance Library

A curated collection of ready-to-use Ordinances. Drop them into your scenario and start playing.

How to use these. Copy the Name and Entry into a new story card. Set Type to Custom and type Ordinance in the field that appears. Add the name as a Trigger keyword. That's it — the Ordinance is live in your scenario.

Combat

Quick Strike
DAMAGING Needs target: Yes
A lightning-fast strike delivered before the opponent can react. Prioritizes speed over power — relies on the element of surprise rather than brute force.
Submitted by: Kael Mikael
Flashing Shadow
DAMAGING Needs target: Yes
A burst of explosive movement closing distance instantly. High risk — if it misses, the caller is left exposed. Roll: 3d6
Submitted by: PrinceF90
Ghost Step
DAMAGING Needs target: Yes
An explosive surge of movement that closes distance instantly, slips into the target's blind spot, and delivers a concentrated strike before they can reorient. Extremely high risk — if the approach is read, the caller is left dangerously overextended and vulnerable to a counter. Roll: 3d6
Submitted by: mirren_sc99
Incapacitating Juggler
DAMAGING Needs target: Yes
A sustained sequence of punches, kicks, and joint locks designed to overwhelm and immobilize. Ends in a pin or takedown. Damage is cumulative — each strike builds on the last. Difficult to escape once the sequence begins.
Submitted by: ITSZ3US
Last Round
DAMAGING Needs target: Yes
The caller expends everything in one final, desperate burst — drawing on reserves of strength or will they shouldn't have left. Hits harder the lower the caller's current HP. At full health this is unremarkable. Near death, it can be catastrophic for the target. The caller cannot use this Ordinance again until they rest.
Submitted by: Rand Lover

Utility & Support

Unwavering Focus
NONDAMAGING Needs target: No
The character clears their mind and centers themselves completely. Distractions fade. Fear subsides. For the next action, the character acts with absolute clarity — their next roll gains advantage regardless of circumstances.
Submitted by: Kael Mikael
Field Patch
NONDAMAGING Needs target: Optional
A quick, practiced field treatment — bandaging, pressure, or improvised medicine applied under fire. Restores a meaningful portion of HP to the target, or the caller if no target is given. Effectiveness depends on available materials and the severity of the wound. Cannot bring someone back from incapacitation — only buys time.
Submitted by: PrinceF90
Rally the Line
NONDAMAGING Needs target: No
The caller issues a sharp command or rallying cry that pulls nearby allies into position. Reinforcements or backup — if any are plausible given the current situation — respond and arrive. The quality and number is up to the AI based on context. Does not work if the caller is isolated or their authority has not been established.
Submitted by: ITSZ3US

Social & Influence

Silver Tongue
NONDAMAGING Needs target: Yes
A carefully crafted appeal — flattery, reason, or manufactured vulnerability — designed to shift the target's disposition toward the caller. The target doesn't need to believe it fully, just enough to lower their guard. On a strong roll, the AI should treat the target as actively cooperative for the remainder of the scene. On a weak roll, they remain skeptical but don't escalate.
Submitted by: Rand Lover
Unnerving Calm
NONDAMAGING Needs target: Yes
The caller says nothing — or very little — and lets the weight of their presence do the work. Deliberate stillness, controlled eye contact, the kind of quiet that makes people feel watched even when they aren't. The target becomes visibly unsettled. On a strong roll, they hesitate, second-guess themselves, or reveal something they didn't intend to. Does not work on targets who have nothing to fear.
Submitted by: Yeet Army

Economy & Negotiation

Hard Bargain
NONDAMAGING Needs target: Yes
The caller presses a merchant, vendor, or trader for a better deal — leveraging information, reputation, implied threat, or sheer persistence. On a strong roll, the AI should reduce the effective price or improve the terms of any trade happening in this scene. On a weak roll, the seller becomes defensive and may refuse to deal further. Does not work in scenarios where pricing is fixed or the seller has no incentive to negotiate.
Submitted by: Rand Lover
