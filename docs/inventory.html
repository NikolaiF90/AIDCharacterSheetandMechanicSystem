System Guide

Inventory System

Track what your character carries — naturally, without breaking immersion.

What Is the Inventory System?

The CSMS inventory system lets you pick up, drop, give, and throw items using natural language commands — no menus, no slashes, no clutter. Your character sheet automatically tracks what you're carrying, and the AI always knows what's in your hands.

Just prefix the inventory action in your input with iv_ and the script handles the rest.

Immersion first. The iv_ prefix is stripped from the story feed before the AI ever sees it. What the AI reads is natural prose — just like you wrote it without the prefix.

Basic Commands

Add iv_ directly before the inventory action in your input. Everything before and after is preserved — only the iv_ part is stripped.

What you typeWhat the AI sees
I walk over and iv_grab the briefcase.I walk over and grab the briefcase.
iv_drop the knife on the floor.drop the knife on the floor.
Alex iv_hand the folder to Marcus.Alex hand the folder to Marcus.
I turn and iv_throw the bottle at the guard.I turn and throw the bottle at the guard.

Keyword List

Any of these words after iv_ will trigger the inventory system:

ActionKeywordsEffect
Taketake, grab, pick up, collect, retrieve, get, acquire, tuckAdds item to your inventory
Dropdrop, discard, throw away, leave, abandon, let go ofRemoves item from your inventory
Givegive, hand, pass, offer, deliver, transfer, slide, shoveTransfers item to another character
Throwthrow, toss, hurl, fling, chuck, lobRemoves item, optional target character

Taking From Another Character

To take an item from a specific character, use from followed by their name:

iv_take documents from Sarah
iv_grab the keycard from the guard
I reach over and iv_take the envelope from Marcus.
The character must have a 📋 sheet. You can only take items from characters tracked by CSMS. NPCs without a character sheet are not tracked — the script won't know what they're carrying.

Giving to Another Character

Use to followed by their name. The item must be in your inventory first:

iv_give the documents to Sarah
I lean across the table and iv_hand the keycard to Marcus.
Always use the character's name — never pronouns. iv_give her the files will fail because "her" is not a character name. Use iv_give files to Sarah instead.

Throwing at a Target

Use at followed by a character name, or throw with no target to just discard:

iv_throw the bottle at Marcus
iv_hurl the knife at the guard
I pull the pin and iv_toss it away.

Throwing removes the item from your inventory regardless of whether it hits. The AI narrates what happens next.

Your Inventory on the Character Sheet

Your character sheet's Notes field holds your inventory. It looks like this:

[Inventory]
- leather jacket
- burner phone
- P99-Pistol
- crumpled note "Meet at pier 7, midnight"

[Ordinances]
-

You can edit this directly — open your 📋 card in the Story Cards panel and add or remove items manually. The script reads it automatically on the next action.

Formatting rules: Each item is one line starting with - . Items with embedded text use quoted format — - note "text here". The quoted text is part of the item name, not a comment.

Item Naming Tips

The script matches items by exact name (case insensitive). What you type to pick something up is what gets stored — and what you need to type to interact with it later.

SituationRecommendation
Items with numbersP99-Pistol, Glock-17 — use hyphens to keep multi-word names together
Multiple of the same itemEach appears as a separate line — - knife twice means two knives
Specific vs genericiv_take knife ≠ iv_take sharp knife — be consistent
Stacking notationbottle x3 works as a name — but you must type it exactly each time

When You Don't Have the Item

If you try to drop, give, or throw something you're not carrying, the script replaces your input with a natural failure — the AI narrates the moment gracefully:

— You type —
I walk to the desk and iv_drop the keycard.

— You don't have it, so the AI sees —
I walk to the desk and reach for the keycard, but realize it isn't there.

— The AI narrates —
Your hand moves to your jacket pocket out of habit, but comes up empty.
The keycard isn't there. You stand at the desk, momentarily at a loss.
Plans fail naturally. The input field is your character's intention. When the inventory check fails, the story reflects that — no immersion broken, no error messages cluttering the feed.

Rules & Limitations

One item at a time

Each iv_ command handles a single item. For multiple items, use separate inputs:

iv_take Glock-17 from Marcus
iv_take P99-Pistol from Marcus

One iv_ per input

Choose the one action that changes inventory. Everything else in your input is preserved as-is:

I grab Marcus by the collar and iv_take his keycard. ✅
iv_take the knife and tuck it into my belt. ✅
iv_take the knife and iv_tuck it into my belt. ✅ (both fire — take + tuck = same item added twice ⚠️)

Receiver must have a character sheet

You can only give to or take from characters with a 📋 sheet. "The receptionist", "the clerk", "the guard" — if they don't have a sheet, they're not tracked.

No pronouns for receivers

iv_give him the gun will fail. iv_give gun to Marcus works. Always use the character's actual name.

Location phrases are stripped automatically

iv_drop wallet on the desk — "on the desk" is stripped from the item name. What gets removed from inventory is "wallet", not "wallet on the desk".
